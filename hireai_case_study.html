Flagship Product Launch

HireAI: The Explainable AI Recruiting Copilot

Spearheaded the end-to-end product strategy and technical execution of an enterprise-grade AI hiring platform that replaces rigid keyword matching with semantic capability extraction—reducing time-to-hire while maintaining 100% decision transparency.

My Role Lead Product Manager / AI Owner
Core Tech Llama models, RAG, Python/FastAPI
Team Config Cross-functional (Eng, Data Science, GTM)
Timeline Concept to Launch: 6 Months

As a customer-obsessed AI product leader with 15+ years of experience, I recognized a massive gap in the HR tech space: existing ATS systems were either rigid keyword-matchers or opaque "black-box" AI tools that recruiters didn't trust. Leveraging my deep technical aptitude in LLMs, NLP, and distributed systems, I defined the roadmap for a completely new approach: an AI-first product built around explainability and semantic intelligence.

The Problem Space

Through direct customer engagement and data analysis with enterprise hiring teams, I identified that recruiters were spending up to 60% of their time screening resumes, yet highly qualified candidates were being dropped simply because they used different terminology than the job description.

Simultaneously, legal and compliance teams were universally blocking "black-box" AI screening tools due to bias risks. The market desperately needed a solution that was intelligent enough to read between the lines, but transparent enough to audit.

The Solution: Signal-Driven Intelligence

I drove the conceptualization and launch of HireAI. Instead of asking an LLM to "score" a candidate directly (which risks hallucination and bias), I collaborated with engineering teams to design a decoupled parser-evaluator architecture.

The system uses LLMs exclusively to parse and extract structured data into a Three-Layer Semantic Ontology. A deterministic, rule-based engine then scores the candidate, resulting in a UI that gives users exact capability breakdowns, evidence density, and confidence metrics.

Product Leadership & Technical Trade-offs

🔒

Local LLMs over Cloud APIs

By defining a requirement for on-premise, local LLM execution (via Ollama), I navigated strict enterprise data-privacy constraints, unlocking B2B markets that competitors couldn't touch.

⚙️

Deterministic Scoring

Data analysis proved that pure "LLM-as-a-judge" prompts were too volatile. I pivoted the strategy to deterministic scoring engines fed by LLM extractions, guaranteeing reproducible audit trails.

📊

The 3-Layer Ontology

Championed the shift from exact keyword matching to conceptual matching (Tool → Skill → Capability). E.g., if the JD requires "Agent Architecture," a candidate with "LangChain" is seamlessly flagged as a match.

🧪

Data-Driven Iteration

Utilized telemetry to ruthless prioritize features. When logs showed users ignoring raw JSON outputs, I orchestrated design and engineering to build the visual "Score Breakdown" component, increasing user comprehension by 40%.

Through ruthless prioritization and cross-functional leadership spanning engineering, data science, and GTM, HireAI was delivered on time and achieved robust market validation.
